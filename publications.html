A physical theory of larval Drosophila behaviour. (2020) Jane Loveless, Alastair Garner, Abdul Raouf Issa, Barbara Webb, Tomoko Ohyama & Claudio R. Alonso. in preparation. A biorxiv pre-print is available here.

This paper represents the culmination of a collaboration with the experimental laboratories of Claudio Alonso and Tomoko Ohyama, at the University of Sussex and Mcgill University, respectively. In the paper, I develop a simple effective theory for larval behaviour from basic symmetry arguments, that predicts/explains features of real larval behaviour surprisingly well. Somewhat bizarrely, the theory is able to capture several behaviours while remaining almost completely agnostic as to their "neural substrates". Furthermore, our analysis shows that several larval behaviours display classical equilibrium (Boltzmann) statistics, with the apparent low dimensionality of behaviour emerging due to Boltzmann suppression of higher frequency postural modes.


A Neuromechanical Model of Larval Chemotaxis (2018) Jane Loveless & Barbara Webb. Integrative and Comparative Biology. Available here.

Modelling the mechanics of exploration in larval Drosophila (2019) Jane Loveless, Konstantinos Lagogiannis & Barbara Webb. PLoS Computational Biology. Available here.

This pair of papers represents the bulk of my PhD research, conducted under the supervision of Barbara Webb and in collaboration with Konstantinos Lagogiannis. In these papers we build a theory of larval exploratory behaviour that is mechanistically grounded in the deterministic chaotic physics of the animal's body. In this theory, exploration emerges as a type of deterministic diffusion process. The second paper introduces sensory cues that can shape the chaotic exploration process to achieve taxis, i.e. to bring the larva towards "favourable" conditions and away from "unfavourable" ones.
