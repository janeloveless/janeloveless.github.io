My name is Dylan Ross. This is my personal website.

I am a PhD student working in Barbara Webb's Insect Robotics Group at the School of Informatics, University of Edinburgh. I am also a member of Adam Stokes' Soft Systems Group at the same University. My research is focused on maggots ( Drosophila melanogaster larvae) and how they crawl around. I use classical physics to describe the body of the maggot and its interaction with the environment, and build mathematical models describing crawling-relevant parts of its brain at varying levels of abstraction.

I am a musician and sound engineer. I usually perform using a prepared guitar and a laptop. My music is close to noise, drone, and post-rock. There is usually a strong emotional component to my music; hopefully that comes across. I previously ran the sound team at a local community arts space, and have occasionally done session studio and external recording work. I've struggled to find time for music while doing my PhD, but recently started to carve out weekends and evenings for practise and recording. I hope to share some of the results here soon.

I once drew a series of comics. If I can find the time, I'd love to write some more and get them up on here.
